Fabrication

Multi-Stage Casting Process

The Hercules alloy is produced through a sequential multi-cast crucible furnace process. Each stage introduces new elements into the melt, working from the outermost trace constituents inward toward the titanium root. Between each cast, the intermediate billet undergoes heat treatment and seasoning before being remelted for the next stage. Temperatures escalate dramatically as the process approaches the refractory tungsten and titanium additions.

1
Cast 1 — Trace Foundation
~420 – 1 495 °C

The outermost trace elements are combined in the first crucible melt. Copper is brought to liquidus first, then Cobalt and Zinc are introduced. The resulting tri-element ingot is poured into the Cast 1 mold and allowed to solidify.

2
Cast 2 — Aluminum Matrix
~660 – 750 °C

The Cast 1 billet is remelted and the 60% aluminum charge is introduced, forming the primary matrix. The combined melt is poured into Cast 2 mold. Seasoning heat treatment is applied to homogenize the aluminum-trace element distribution.

Cast 1 BilletAl 60%
3
Cast 3 — Tungsten Integration
~3 400+ °C

Temperature ramps sharply to accommodate the 4% tungsten addition. The Cast 2 billet is remelted at extreme temperature and tungsten feedstock is dissolved under controlled atmosphere. This is the most thermally demanding stage.

Cast 2 BilletW 4%
4
Cast 4 — Titanium Root
~1 668+ °C

The 30% titanium base — the root ingredient — is the final elemental addition. The Cast 3 billet is remelted and titanium is integrated, forming the Ti-Al dual-phase backbone. Poured into Cast 4 mold with final seasoning treatment.

Cast 3 BilletTi 30%
M
Master Cast — Final Form

All six elements unified. The final billet is remelted one last time and cast into the Hercules hammer head geometry. Post-cast finishing includes precision machining and surface treatment.

Seasoning Framework

Each intermediate casting stage incorporates a proprietary seasoning protocol — a controlled heat treatment cycle applied between successive melts. The seasoning process uses specialized companion alloy formulations unique to each stage to condition the billet microstructure before the next element is introduced. This iterative temper-remelt-add cycle ensures optimal grain boundary integration and phase coherence across all six constituent elements. The elementary seasoning framework is shared across DART Meadow’s advanced alloy programs.
